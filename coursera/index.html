Coursera

YAY! I Finished My Coursera Course
My Web Developer Certificate from Johns Hopkins University via Coursera

Well...what I can say? I finished the course with a few days to spare :P and I had a fun time going through the tutorials. What I liked was that the videos made it that much more helpful with the addition of being able to save the audio text to a personal file along with the timestamp for where the audio / text was located. A really good feature. That allowed me to go back and notate what I missed. Along the way, I documented my learning and work. The likelihood of me going over the course again is high because I made some really good notes. Also, Yaakov Chaikin (the instructor) did a great job explaining HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

I'd love to make a learning system like the one Coursea was using. I would probably add a way to save out as an archive but at this point, the course did what I needed to do. Overall, I was very happy with the refresher and I did learn stuff I either forgot about or didn't know at all. While I know I've done the work I just didn't know what they were called. Ahhh yes, the benefits of formalized learning.

What would I like to see done differently? I think the last module was more difficult than it should have been. I found the explanation a bit difficult to follow and decipher. However, after a few minutes, I figured what it was they needed done. Following someone else's code and work is a job in itself but overall I managed to do what was required and also fashioned an alternate solution. You know, to make things more difficult for myself? lol

As a next step, I will most likely head on over to another learning site to see what else I can enjoy. Thanks for dropping by and happy programming!
